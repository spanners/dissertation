If you want to take a look at some of the research on regular languages and Algol, go to

http://www.cs.bath.ac.uk/~gam23/papers.html

and scroll down to the section "Regular languages and game semantics."

There you will find:
  • The first paper we wrote on this, called "Reasoning about Idealised Algol using regular languages"
  • A longer version of that, probably a bit less good, but perhaps containing some better introductory parts, called "The regular language semantics of second-order Idealised Algol"
  • Some lecture slides on game semantics (which is what underlies this work) and the regular language interpretation of programs, which might be accessible but might omit too much background.
I think you have already taken a quick look at most of these but anyway, they are a starting point. Feel free to contact me at any point if you want to talk about things, otherwise see you next Wednesday.
